B. Date

题目描述

::::: epigraph One and another moment accumulated will become a whole life.

:::: epigraph-source

  • -- Tomori

Looking at Anon, Tomori asked sincerely, "Can you form a band with me for your whole life?" But Anon did not reply. After returning home sadly, Tomori still wanted to know how long she could form a band with Anon, so she found a string consisting of numbers from 0 to 9 and counted the number of all the subsequences of legal dates in it, which she believes to be the number of days she can form a band with Anon. However, counting subsequences isn't a simple job, so Tomori asks you to help her find this answer.

{style="width:500px;height:auto;"}Tomori asking Anon to form a band with her whole life.

Formally, you are given a string S \textstyle S S consisting of only numbers from 0 to 9, and you need to count all the subsequences in it which are legal dates. Since the answer may be very big, you only need to output the answer modulo 998244353 \textstyle 998244353 998244353.

A subsequence of S \textstyle S S is obtained by deleting some number of elements (eventually, also zero) from S \textstyle S S, leaving the remaining elements in their original order.

A legal date T \textstyle T T is defined as follows:

  • ∣ T ∣ = 8 \textstyle |T| = 8 ∣T∣=8.
  • The first four numbers in T \textstyle T T denote the year Y \textstyle Y Y, then the following two numbers denote the month M \textstyle M M, then the following two numbers denote the day D \textstyle D D. For example, a legal date T \textstyle T T with Y = 2025 , M = 4 , D = 20 \textstyle Y = 2025, M=4, D=20 Y=2025,M=4,D=20 is 20250420 \textstyle 20250420 20250420, and a legal date T \textstyle T T with Y = 1 , M = 1 , D = 1 \textstyle Y = 1, M=1, D=1 Y=1,M=1,D=1 is 00010101 \textstyle 00010101 00010101.
  • The year Y \textstyle Y Y should satisfy 1 ≤ Y ≤ 9999 \textstyle 1 \le Y \le 9999 1≤Y≤9999, and there are no further constraints on M \textstyle M M and D \textstyle D D except that the date must be a real, valid date.

In this problem, we use the common rules of leap year. A year is called a leap year if and only if

  • The year is divisible by 4 \textstyle 4 4, and;
  • The year is not divisible by 100 \textstyle 100 100 unless it is also divisible by 400 \textstyle 400 400.

Otherwise, the year is called a common year. For example, 2000 was a leap year, but 1900 was a common year. The number of days in each month is shown as the following table.

::: center Month 01 02 03 04 05 06 07 08 09 10 11 12

Days (Common Year) 31 28 31 30 31 30 31 31 30 31 30 31 Days (Leap Year) 31 29 31 30 31 30 31 31 30 31 30 31

输入描述

The first line of input contains a single integer n ( 1 ≤ n ≤ 10 5 ) \textstyle n (1 \le n \le 10^5) n(1≤n≤105) --- the length of S \textstyle S S.

The second line contains a string S \textstyle S S of length n \textstyle n n, consisting of only numbers from 0 to 9.

输出描述

Output a single integer, which is the number of days Tomori can form a band with Anon, modulo 998244353 \textstyle 998244353 998244353.
