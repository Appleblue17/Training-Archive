K. Amazing Sets (amazing)

题目描述

You are given a rooted tree T T T of n n n vertices and n − 1 n-1 n−1 directed edges.For vertex i i i, its value is a i a_i ai​. Vertex x x x is reachable from vertex y y y iff there exists a path that starts from y y y, ends at x x x, and only goes through directed edges. It is guaranteed that all vertices are reachable from the root.Given m m m pairs of vertices ( u i , v i ) (u_i,v_i) (ui​,vi​), satisfying that u i u_i ui​ is reachable from v i v_i vi​ on the original tree. Now, new directed edges u i → v i u_i\to v_i ui​→vi​ will be added to form a new graph.Define a subset S S S (may be empty) of the set of the tree vertices amazing, iff for each element x x x in S S S, all the vertices reachable from x x x in the new graph are in S S S. The value of an **amazing **set S S S is defined as the sum of the values of all elements in S S S. The value of an empty set is 0 0 0.For all **amazing **sets, how many different kinds of values appeared?

输入描述

The first line includes a single integer n n n ( 1 ≤ n ≤ 10 4 1\le n\le 10^4 1≤n≤104), which is the number of vertices of tree T T T.The second line includes n n n integers. The i i i-th number represents a i a_i ai​ ( 0 ≤ a i ≤ ∑ i = 1 n a i ≤ 10 4 0\le a_i\le \sum_{i=1}^n a_i\le 10^4 0≤ai​≤∑i=1n​ai​≤104).The next n − 1 n-1 n−1 lines show all n − 1 n-1 n−1 directed edges of the tree. For the i i i-th line in this part, two given numbers are U i , V i U_i,V_i Ui​,Vi​ ( 1 ≤ U i , V i ≤ n 1\le U_i,V_i\le n 1≤Ui​,Vi​≤n) respectively. An edge starting from U i U_i Ui​ and ending at V i V_i Vi​ exists.The next line has an integer m m m ( 1 ≤ m ≤ 5 × 10 5 1\le m\le 5\times 10^5 1≤m≤5×105), representing the number of new edges.The next m m m lines show all m m m new directed edges of the tree. For the i i i-th line in this part, two given numbers are u i , v i u_i,v_i ui​,vi​ ( 1 ≤ u i , v i ≤ n 1\le u_i,v_i\le n 1≤ui​,vi​≤n) respectively. A new edge starting from u i u_i ui​ and ending at v i v_i vi​ exists.

输出描述

A single number which is the answer to the question above.

1 2 3

1

3

说明

There are three amazing sets in the sample: { 1 , 2 , 3 } \lbrace 1,2,3\rbrace {1,2,3}, { 3 } \lbrace 3\rbrace {3}, and ∅ \varnothing ∅. Since they have different values, the answer is 3 3 3.
