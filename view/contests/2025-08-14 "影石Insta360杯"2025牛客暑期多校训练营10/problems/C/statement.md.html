C. Hexagis (hexagis)

题目描述

Here are some definitions before the problem statement.

  • Two squares in the square grid are adjacent iff they share exactly one side.
  • Two squares in the square grid are reachable iff there exists a sequence of squares, where the two squares are the first and the last term of the sequence respectively, and each pair of adjacent squares in the sequence is adjacent in the square grid.
  • A subset of the square grid is 4-connected iff every pair of squares in the subset is reachable in the subset after deleting all squares not included in the subset. A 4-connected subset is also called a block.

There are seven kinds of tetrominoes in the popular game 'tetris'. They are all the possible shapes of four 4-connected squares in the square grid, where shapes that are able to coincide after some translation and rotation are considered the same.

{style="height:auto;width:240px;"}

(seven kinds of tetrominoes in 'tetris')

However, you consider 'tetris' too easy. That's because there are few kinds of tetrominoes used in 'tetris'. As a result, few combinations of tetrominoes can be formed, making the game quite simple. Now, you've made up your mind to play a similar but more difficult game: 'hexagis'.There are 60 60 60 kinds of hexominoes in the game 'hexagis'. They are all the possible shapes of six 4-connected squares in the square grid, where shapes that are able to coincide after some translation and rotation are considered the same.The goal is to fit these hexominoes in a 19 × 19 19 \times 19 19×19 square grid, using exactly one hexomino of each kind. At the same time, every square except the central square should be covered by exactly one hexomino.This game is significantly harder, as you scratched your head but found no solution. So you decided to share the game with your friend. To your surprise, he claimed that he solved the game as soon as he saw it. To prove his words, he gave you T T T solutions he found. Unfortunately, you discovered that all of his solutions were wrong by finding out how many hexominoes of each kind were used in each of his solutions.Now he begins to believe that no solution exists. Can you find one to prove him wrong?

输入描述

The input contains  19 T + 1 19T+1 19T+1 lines:

  • The first line contains  T T T ( 1 ≤ T ≤ 500 1\le T\le500 1≤T≤500), indicating the number of your friend's solutions.
  • After the first line follow  19 T 19T 19T lines, the  19 k + i − 18 19k+i-18 19k+i−18 -th ( 1 ≤ k ≤ T , 1 ≤ i ≤ 19 1\le k\le T,1\le i\le19 1≤k≤T,1≤i≤19) line of the input contains a string  S k , i S_{k,i} Sk,i​ consisting of  19 19 19 characters, indicating the  i i i -th row of the  k k k -th solution of your friend.

In the  k k k -th solution, the tenth character of  S k , 10 S_{k,10} Sk,10​ ( 1 ≤ k ≤ T 1\le k\le T 1≤k≤T) is '.', while the rest of the characters are number digits. 4-connected characters with the same number represent a block of your friend's solution.

输出描述

Your output should contain  T + 19 T+19 T+19 lines:

  • If the  k k k -th ( 1 ≤ k ≤ T 1\le k\le T 1≤k≤T) solution of your friend contains a block that is not a hexomino, output  − 1 -1 −1 in the  k k k -th line. Otherwise, output  60 60 60 non-negative integers  a k , 1 , a k , 2 , a k , 3 , ⋯   , a k , 60 a_{k,1},a_{k,2},a_{k,3},\cdots,a_{k,60} ak,1​,ak,2​,ak,3​,⋯,ak,60​, indicating the numbers of the  60 60 60 types of hexominoes used in the solution in non-increasing order.
  • Then you should output  19 19 19 more lines, the  T + i T+i T+i -th line in your output ( 1 ≤ i ≤ 19 1\le i\le19 1≤i≤19) should contain a string  R i R_i Ri​ consisting of  19 19 19 characters, indicating the  i i i -th row of your solution.

You have to make sure that the tenth character of  R 10 R_{10} R10​ is '.', while the rest of the characters are number digits. 4-connected characters with the same number represent a block of your solution. Any output violating the form above or not satisfying the requirements of a real solution will be considered as Wrong Answer.If multiple solutions exist, output any.
