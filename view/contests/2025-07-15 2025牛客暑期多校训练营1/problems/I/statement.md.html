I. Iron Bars Cutting

题目描述

There are n n n iron bars, where the length of the i i i-th iron bar is a i a_i ai​. These n n n iron bars are welded together in the order of 1 , 2 , 3 , … , n 1, 2, 3, \ldots, n 1,2,3,…,n to form a very long iron bar for some usage. Welding two adjacent iron bars creates a weld point, resulting in a total of n − 1 n-1 n−1 weld points.

Little Q needs to cut this long iron bar back into n n n iron bars. Each time he can choose an iron bar that has at least one weld point and select a weld point to cut the iron bar into two at that weld point, then let the lengths of the resulting two iron bars be l 1 l_1 l1​ and l 2 l_2 l2​. The imbalance of this cut is defined as ∣ l 1 − l 2 ∣ |l_1 - l_2| ∣l1​−l2​∣, and the cost of the cut is defined as min ⁡ { l 1 , l 2 } × ⌈ log ⁡ 2 ( l 1 + l 2 ) ⌉ \min\{l_1,l_2\} \times \lceil \log_2(l_1+l_2) \rceil min{l1​,l2​}×⌈log2​(l1​+l2​)⌉. Note that ∣ x ∣ |x| ∣x∣ is the absolute value of x x x, and ⌈ log ⁡ 2 ( y ) ⌉ \lceil \log_2(y) \rceil ⌈log2​(y)⌉ is the smallest integer z z z such that 2 z ≥ y 2^z \ge y 2z≥y.

Little Q hopes that the imbalances of the n − 1 n-1 n−1 cuts, denoted as b 1 , b 2 , … , b n − 1 b_1, b_2, \ldots, b_{n-1} b1​,b2​,…,bn−1​, satisfy b 1 ≥ b 2 ≥ ⋯ ≥ b n − 1 b_1 \ge b_2 \ge \cdots \ge b_{n-1} b1​≥b2​≥⋯≥bn−1​, and the total cost of these n − 1 n-1 n−1 cuts is minimized. You need to find the minimum total cost for the first cut at the weld point between the i i i-th and ( i + 1 i+1 i+1)-th iron bars, or indicate if it is impossible to cut out n n n iron bars, for each i = 1 , 2 , … , n − 1 i=1,2,\ldots,n-1 i=1,2,…,n−1.
